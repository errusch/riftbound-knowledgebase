# Spiritforged Sealed 101: Your Pre-Rift Survival Guide

So you're headed to a Pre-Rift event for Spiritforged. Smart move—prerelease sealed is the best way to experience a new set before the meta gets solved. But if you show up thinking sealed is just "open packs and hope," you're going to get demolished by players who actually know what they're doing.

This guide will get you ready to build a real deck, not a pile.

## How Sealed Works (The Basics)

You get six booster packs. You open them, spread out your cards, and build a 40-card deck from what you opened. That's it. No trading, no borrowing, no "I forgot my Fury cards can I use yours?"

**The golden rules:**
- **40 cards minimum.** Not 41. Not 43. Forty. Every card over 40 reduces your chances of drawing your best cards.
- **17 lands is the default.** You might go 16 or 18 depending on your curve, but start at 17.
- **23 playables.** That's what's left after lands. Quality varies wildly in sealed—you'll play cards you'd never touch in constructed.

Unlike Draft, you're not competing for cards during the pick. Your pool is your pool. The challenge is building the best deck from random chaos.

## General Sealed Strategy

### Bombs Win Games

In sealed, bombs—your rares and epics that just end games if unanswered—are king. A single Spiritforged legend can carry a mediocre deck to 3-0. If you open a bomb, you build around it. End of story.

**Bomb evaluation checklist:**
- Does it win the game if your opponent can't answer it immediately?
- Does it generate value even if removed?
- Is it hard to answer with common/uncommon removal?

If you answered yes to two or more, that's your first-pick card. Splash it if you have to.

### Removal is Currency

Removal spells are the second most important cards in your pool. Sealed games go long, and whoever can answer threats wins. Combat tricks are cute; hard removal is what matters.

**Prioritize unconditional removal.** "Destroy target creature" beats "deal 3 damage" every time. In Spiritforged, watch for removal that hits Equipment too—it's two-for-one value when your opponent's stacking their Champion.

Common/uncommon removal defines your deck's ceiling. You can't rely on bombs alone.

### Mana Curve: The Boring Thing That Wins

Your curve is the distribution of cards by mana cost. In sealed, you want:
- **2-drop:** 2-3 creatures (you need early plays)
- **3-drop:** 4-5 creatures (your bread and butter)
- **4-drop:** 3-4 creatures (this is where you start winning)
- **5+ drops:** 3-4 bombs and finishers

If your deck is all 5+ mana, you'll die before you cast anything. If it's all 2-3 drops, you'll run out of gas. Balance wins.

**The test:** Lay out your playables by mana cost. If any column looks empty or overcrowded, fix it before you sleeve up.

### Domain Consistency Beats Greed

Two-domain decks are the default. Three-domain is doable if you have fixing (Gold tokens help). Four-domain is asking to lose to your own mana base.

**Don't get cute.** If you have a deep pool in two domains, stay there. Splashing a third domain for one bomb is fine *if* you have Gold tokens or dual-land effects. Otherwise, cut it and play consistent cards.

## Spiritforged-Specific Strategy

### Equipment Is Everything (And It Changed)

Spiritforged's main mechanic is Equipment, and if you're coming from Origins, **forget what you know.** The Equipment rules changed between sets, and understanding the new system is the difference between winning and watching your opponent snowball.

**Key Equipment changes to know:**
- Equipment now attaches at instant speed if the trigger allows it
- Unequipping is easier, meaning your opponent can dodge removal more effectively
- Equipment buffs stack harder with Spiritforged Champions

What this means for sealed: **Equipment-based strategies are way stronger.** If you open Equipment and Champions that synergize, that's your deck. Don't ignore the uncommon Equipment—some of them are bombs in the right shell.

**Equipment evaluation:**
- Does it protect your creature or give evasion? (Value tier 1)
- Does it have an enters-the-battlefield effect? (Free value even if removed)
- Does it cost 3+ mana to equip? (Probably too slow unless the payoff is massive)

Removal that destroys Equipment is premium. Run it.

### Gold Tokens: Your Mana Fixing and Late-Game Engine

Gold tokens are Spiritforged's resource mechanic, and they're *way* more important than they look. Each Gold token can be sacrificed for one mana of any domain.

**Why Gold tokens matter in sealed:**
- **They fix your mana.** Splashing that third-domain bomb? Gold tokens make it consistent.
- **They smooth your curve.** Ramp from 3 to 5 when you need it.
- **They're late-game value.** That 6-mana finisher becomes castable on turn 4.

**How to maximize Gold:**
- Cards that generate multiple Gold tokens are first-picks (yes, even at uncommon)
- Don't spend Gold early unless you have to—save them for when it matters
- If you're three-domain, Gold tokens are mandatory

Some players treat Gold like Treasure tokens in other games—disposable early ramp. That's wrong. Gold is your mid-to-late game insurance. Spend wisely.

### Combat Math Gets Weird

Spiritforged has more instant-speed interaction than Origins. Equipment can shift mid-combat. Gold tokens enable surprise plays. Your opponent representing two mana might actually be representing five.

**Play around it:**
- Don't attack into open mana unless you can afford to lose the creature
- Equipment means creatures are bigger than they look—add +2/+2 to any creature that could equip
- Gold tokens = instant-speed tricks. If they have Gold, they have options.

The best sealed players are patient. Don't overextend into a board wipe you can't see coming.

## Final Tips for Pre-Rift Day

**Open your packs slowly.** Lay out your pool by domain and rarity. Build your bombs-and-removal pile first, then fill in the curve.

**Don't be afraid to rebuild.** If your first build feels clunky, tear it down and start over. You have time.

**Sideboarding matters.** Sealed is best-of-three. If your opponent is Equipment-heavy, bring in your artifact removal. If they're slow, bring in your aggro cards.

**Play tight.** Sealed punishes mistakes harder than Draft. Every card matters when you only have 23 playables.

Now go build something nasty and take home that Pre-Rift promo. You got this.
