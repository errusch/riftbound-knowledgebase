# The Complete Spiritforged Budget Deck Guide: Champion Decks to Competitive Play

**Last Updated:** February 11, 2026

Breaking into Riftbound doesn't require breaking the bank. With **Spiritforged (SFD)** introducing new champions and powerful equipment synergies, budget players have more competitive entry points than ever. This guide covers everything from $20-30 Champion Deck starting points to upgrade paths that can compete at local tournaments.

## Budget Tiers in Riftbound

What "budget" actually means in practice:

- **Ultra-Budget ($20-30):** Champion Deck + a few singles
- **Budget ($40-60):** Optimized single or dual-domain builds using commons/uncommons
- **Budget-Competitive ($80-100):** Tournament-viable decks with some rare/epic inclusions
- **Premium ($120+):** Full competitive lists with optimal cards

Most players start with a Champion Deck and upgrade gradually. The important part is knowing which Champion Decks have the best foundation and which upgrades give you the most value per dollar.

## Champion Deck Entry Points ($20-30)

### Sivir Spiritforged Champion Deck
**Domain:** Chaos (Purple)  
**Price Range:** $25-30  
**Competitive Ceiling:** Medium-High

**Why Start Here:**
Sivir, Mercenary (SFD-143/221) brings aggressive tempo and equipment synergies that scale well with budget upgrades. The Chaos domain focuses on card selection, mobility, and discard synergies, so you can build powerful lines with commons and uncommons.

**Core Strategy:**
Sivir's [Accelerate] ability lets you pay 1 energy + 1 Chaos rune to have her enter ready, immediately threatening battlefields. When you've spent 2+ runes in a turn, she gains +2 Might and [Ganking] (battlefield-to-battlefield movement), making her hard to pin down.

**Out-of-the-Box Strengths:**
- Strong common base with Treasure Hunter (SFD-130/221) generating Gold tokens
- Natural equipment synergies from Spiritforged commons
- Card selection spells like Stacked Deck (OGN-183/298) improve consistency
- Mobility gameplay teaches positioning fundamentals

**Immediate Upgrade Path ($10-15):**
- 3x Treasure Trove (OGN-186/298) - Uncommon - ~$3-4 total
- 2x Switcheroo (SFD-145/221) - Rare - ~$6-8 total
- Additional Chaos domain removal spells

### Soraka Spiritforged Champion Deck
**Domain:** Order (Yellow)  
**Price Range:** $25-30  
**Competitive Ceiling:** Medium

**Why Start Here:**
Soraka, Wanderer (SFD-173/221) offers a defensive control strategy with built-in unit protection. Order domain's stun effects and revival mechanics provide solid board control. Good for players who prefer reactive gameplay.

**Core Strategy:**
Soraka must be assigned combat damage last and can save other units from death by healing, exhausting, and recalling them if they have less Might than her. This creates a defensive shell that buys time to assemble late-game plays.

**Out-of-the-Box Strengths:**
- Solari Shieldbearer (OGN-051/298) and stun effects control tempo
- Revival and resurrection effects enable grindy matchups
- Spectral Matron (OGN-226/298) brings back units from trash
- Teaches resource management and defensive timing

**Immediate Upgrade Path ($10-15):**
- 3x Trifarian Gloryseeker (OGN-217/298) - Common - ~$3 total
- 2x Solari Chief (OGN-225/298) - Uncommon - ~$4-5 total
- Symbol of the Solari (OGN-227/298) for combat tricks

## Budget Build Archetypes ($40-60)

### Chaos Aggro-Mobility
**Core Champions:** Sivir, Mercenary  
**Price:** $45-55  
**Competitive Rating:** 7/10 at FNM-level events

**Decklist Core:**
- **Champion:** 1x Sivir, Mercenary (SFD-143/221) - Purple - Rare
- **Aggressive Units:**
  - 3x Treasure Hunter (SFD-130/221) - Purple - Common
  - 3x Sneaky Deckhand (OGN-176/298) - Purple - Common
  - 3x Stealthy Pursuer (OGN-177/298) - Purple - Common
  - 2x Traveling Merchant (OGN-185/298) - Purple - Uncommon
- **Equipment Package:**
  - 3x Treasure Trove (OGN-186/298) - Purple - Uncommon
  - 2x Gold Token generators from Treasure Hunter triggers
- **Spells:**
  - 3x Stacked Deck (OGN-183/298) - Purple - Uncommon
  - 2x Switcheroo (SFD-145/221) - Purple - Rare
- **Battlefields:**
  - 2x Treasure Hoard (SFD-220/221) - Gray - Uncommon

**Why It Works:**
This build maximizes Sivir's [Ganking] ability by maintaining consistent 2+ rune spending through cheap, efficient units and equipment. Treasure Hunter generates Gold tokens that ramp your resources while Stacked Deck filters for your best cards. Sneaky Deckhand can deploy directly to open battlefields for immediate pressure.

**Upgrade Priority:**
1. Add 2x Soulgorger (OGN-196/298) - Rare - for recursion (~$8-10)
2. Include 1-2x Super Mega Death Rocket! (OGN-252/298) - Epic - for reach (~$12-15)
3. Upgrade to showcase Sivir if you want (optional)

### Order Control-Stun
**Core Champions:** Soraka, Wanderer  
**Price:** $50-60  
**Competitive Rating:** 6/10 at FNM-level events

**Decklist Core:**
- **Champion:** 1x Soraka, Wanderer (SFD-173/221) - Yellow - Rare

  - 3x Stand United (OGN-053/298) - Green - Common (buff synergy)

  - 2x Trifarian War Camp (OGN-294/298) - Gray - Uncommon
  - 1x Targon's Peak (OGN-289/298) - Gray - Uncommon

**Why It Works:**
This build leverages Order and Calm (Green) synergies for maximum defensive value. Multiple stun effects control enemy units while Soraka's protection ability keeps your board alive. Spectral Matron recursion and Solari Shrine card draw grind out wins.

**Upgrade Priority:**
1. Add 2x Taric, Protector (OGN-074/298) - Rare - for [Shield] synergy (~$10-12)
2. Include Sterak's Gage (SFD-056/221) - Rare - for Quick-Draw protection (~$8-10)
3. Add premium removal or combat tricks based on your local meta

## Competitive Budget Builds ($80-100)

Once you've tested a budget build and know your preferred playstyle, it's time to push toward competitive viability. At this price point, you're adding powerful rares and select epics that define top-tier strategies.

### Chaos Equipment Rush
**Price:** $90-100  
**Tournament Viability:** Can top-8 local tournaments with tight play

**Key Additions:**
- 2x Spinning Axe (SFD-186/221) - Epic Signature - Fury/Chaos (~$15-18 total)
- 1x Super Mega Death Rocket! (OGN-252/298) - Epic Signature - Fury/Chaos (~$8-10)
- 2x Soulgorger (OGN-196/298) - Rare (~$10-12 total)
- 3x Switcheroo (SFD-145/221) - Rare (~$12-15 total)

Spinning Axe's [Quick-Draw] + [Temporary] creates explosive turns where you attach 3 Might to a unit immediately, push damage, then recycle the axe for future use. Soulgorger enables late-game recursion by replaying units from trash for free (minus Power cost).

**Meta Positioning:**
Strong against midrange. Gets crushed by heavy control with mass removal. Sideboard anti-control cards for Game 2/3.

### Order Revival Control
**Price:** $85-95  
**Tournament Viability:** Strong in creature-heavy metas

**Key Additions:**
- 2x Taric, Protector (OGN-074/298) - Rare (~$12-15 total)
- 1x Svellsongur (SFD-059/221) - Epic Equipment (~$10-12)
- 2x Sterak's Gage (SFD-056/221) - Rare (~$10-12 total)
- Additional stun effects and board wipes

Svellsongur copies the text of the unit it's attached to, effectively doubling your champion's abilities or key unit effects. This creates powerful recursive loops when combined with Spectral Matron and Soraka's protection.

**Meta Positioning:**
Good against aggro and midrange. Struggles against combo decks that ignore the board. Requires tight gameplay and careful resource tracking.

## Are These Actually Competitive?

**Short Answer:** Chaos Aggro-Mobility and Order Revival Control can both compete at local tournaments with proper sideboarding and practice.

**What Makes Budget Decks Succeed:**
1. **Meta Knowledge:** Budget decks succeed by targeting prevalent archetypes. If your local meta is heavy on creature combat, Order stun-lock performs well.
2. **Tight Play:** Budget builds have less margin for error. Sequencing Sivir's rune spending or timing Soraka's unit saves correctly is mandatory, not optional.
3. **Sideboard Budget:** Save $15-20 of your budget for sideboard hate cards. Budget decks often win through Game 2/3 adaptation.

**Realistic Expectations:**
- **FNM/Locals (20-30 players):** Top-8 achievable with either build
- **Regional Qualifiers (50+ players):** Top-16 possible with good play
- **Nationals/Major Events:** Don't expect to top-8 without premium cards, but you'll win games

## Upgrade Paths: From $30 to Competitive

### The 3-Month Plan
**Month 1 ($30 starting):** Purchase Champion Deck, add $15 of key commons/uncommons  
**Month 2 ($60 total):** Add 2-3 important rares that define your strategy  
**Month 3 ($90-100 total):** Include 1-2 epics and optimize manabase/battlefields

This gradual approach lets you test cards before committing to expensive pieces. A lot of players discover they prefer a different archetype after piloting their first build—don't over-invest early.

### Smart Shopping Tips
- **Buy commons/uncommons in bulk lots:** $15-20 gets you playsets of most non-chase commons
- **Trade up:** Convert unused rares into cards you need via local trade networks
- **Watch for set rotation:** Older sets often drop in price when new releases arrive
- **Proxy for testing:** Use printed proxies at home to test expensive cards before buying

## Budget Deck Piloting Tips

**Mulligan Decisions:**
- Keep hands with 2-3 land equivalents (runes/battlefields) and a 2-3 energy curve
- Mulligan hands with only 5+ energy cards even if they're powerful
- In budget builds, card quality matters less than curve consistency

**Resource Management:**
- Track your rune spending carefully for Sivir's [Ganking] trigger
- Don't over-commit to one battlefield. Budget decks lack expensive haymakers to come back from blowouts.
- Save 1-2 energy for reactive spells in midgame turns

**Combat Math:**
Budget decks win through incremental advantages. Calculate exact damage to avoid trading down. Use mobility and positioning to force unfavorable blocks.

## Closing Thoughts

Riftbound's Spiritforged expansion makes budget competitive play real. Champion Decks are legitimate competitive foundations, not just casual intro products. With $30-40 of targeted upgrades, you're sitting across from premium decks with a real shot to win.

Start with Sivir if you like aggressive, proactive play. Choose Soraka if you prefer reactive, controlling gameplay. Both paths lead to competitive budget builds under $100 that can top-8 local events.

The trick isn't spending more—it's spending smart, learning your deck cold, and adapting to your local meta.
