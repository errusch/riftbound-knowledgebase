# Community Deck Vault

## Browse Battle-Tested Decklists

The Community Deck Vault is your competitive edge. Here you'll find tournament-proven decklists, innovative brews, and meta-defining strategies submitted by players across the Riftbound competitive scene. Every deck is tested in real matches, not theorycrafted in a vacuum.

Use the filters to search by domain, archetype, format, or tournament placement. Sort by rating to find the most battle-tested builds, or browse recent submissions to catch emerging strategies before they hit the mainstream. Each decklist includes full card details with set codes, collector numbers, and rarity—everything you need to build and pilot it immediately.

This isn't a repository for janky pet decks. Submissions are vetted by the community through upvotes and performance tracking. High-rated decks have proven themselves in actual competitive play. Low-rated decks either haven't been tested enough or couldn't deliver results. The system is self-correcting: strong decks rise, weak decks fade.

Whether you're prepping for your first tournament or refining a championship list, the Vault gives you access to real competitive intelligence. Learn from other players' successes and failures. Test their builds, adapt their tech choices, and contribute your own innovations back to the community.

This is the competitive metagame in real time. Use it.

## How to Submit a Deck

**Requirements:**
- Minimum 5 ranked matches or 1 tournament placement with the deck
- Complete 60-card maindeck + sideboard (if applicable)
- Full card details: set code, collector number, rarity, and domain color
- Archetype tag and brief strategy description (2-3 sentences)

**Submission Process:**
1. Log in with your Hextech Analytics account
2. Click "Submit Deck" in the Vault header
3. Enter your decklist using the provided format template
4. Add archetype, format, and tournament results (if any)
5. Include a brief explanation of your strategy and key matchups
6. Submit for community review

**Card Format (MANDATORY):**
Every card must follow this format:
`[quantity] [card name] - [set] - [color] - [rarity] - [card number]`

Example: `3x Sett, Brawler - SFD - Orange - Epic - 042/280`

Use exact card names from the Riftcodex database. Include domain color for every card—it's the fastest visual lookup for most players.

## What Makes a Good Submission

**Strong submissions include:**
- **Real match data:** Tournament placement, ranked win rate, or match count
- **Detailed strategy notes:** Core gameplan, key combos, mulligan priorities
- **Matchup breakdown:** What you beat, what beats you, and why
- **Sideboard guide:** What to bring in/out against top archetypes
- **Tech choices explained:** Why you're running specific 1-ofs or unusual cards

**Weak submissions that get downvoted:**
- Untested "big brain" theorycrafts with 0 games played
- Incomplete decklists missing card details or sideboard
- No explanation of strategy or how to pilot the deck
- Clickbait titles with no substance ("THIS DECK BREAKS THE META!!!")
- Copy-pasted lists from other sources without attribution or testing

The community values **honesty and rigor**. If your deck went 2-3 at FNM, say so. If it's 15-5 in ranked, show the data. Players respect transparency and actual results over hype.

## Rating & Voting System

**How It Works:**
- Every registered user can upvote or downvote submitted decks
- Your vote reflects whether the deck **delivers on its claims**
- Upvote = deck performs as advertised, well-explained, competitively viable
- Downvote = deck underperforms, poorly explained, or untested theory

**Rating Formula:**
Deck rating = (Upvotes - Downvotes) + Performance Bonus

**Performance Bonus:**
- Top 8 tournament finish: +10 rating
- Top 16: +5 rating
- 60%+ win rate over 20+ matches: +5 rating
- Tournament win: +25 rating

High ratings mean the community has validated both the deck's performance and the quality of the submission. A 5-star brew with detailed matchup notes is more valuable than a raw tournament list with zero explanation.

**Fraud Prevention:**
- Vote manipulation (alt accounts, brigading) results in permanent ban
- Submissions with fabricated tournament results are removed
- Repeated low-effort submissions result in submission privileges revoked

**Sorting Options:**
- **Top Rated:** Highest community score (upvotes + performance)
- **Recent:** Newest submissions first (catch emerging meta shifts)
- **Trending:** Decks gaining upvotes rapidly (hot new tech)
- **Tournament Proven:** Filter by verified tournament placements only

Use the rating system honestly. Upvote decks that help you improve. Downvote decks that waste your time. The Vault is only as good as the community makes it.
